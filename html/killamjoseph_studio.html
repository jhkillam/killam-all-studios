Studio

My studio setup has evolved significantly over the years. Pictured below is my current setup which I run on a Windows 10 desktop PC that I built myself. Key hardware and software for my workflow includes:

  • Fractal Audio Axe-FX II XL+
  • Presonus 18|24 I/O
  • Presonus Eris Monitors
  • REAPER
  • Massive Soundworks IRs
  • Plugin Alliance
  • GetGood Drums
  • Solemn Tones
  • NeuralDSP
  • Waves
  • Izotope
  • Oeksound
  • Wavesfactory

Here's a fun trip down memory lane taking a look at my previous setups over the years.

2000

At the point this picture was taken (which might not actually have been the year 2000), I'd been poking around on my dad's Mac PowerPC system running Pro Tools for a few years. Along with my buddies Jack & Tink, we tracked a punk album all by ourself over the course of a weekend.

2006

Venturing out on my own meant I couldn't rely on my dad's equipment anymore for recording, and after experimenting with a simple 8 track digital recorder I quickly realized Pro Tools was the only way for me to really get the type of results I wanted. I sold the 8 track and upgraded to a MacBook Pro with a DIGI002 I/O, pictured above mixing something in the classic U-Haul storage shed jam space.

2009

Finally a (tiny) desk of my own! My wife and I moved to Houston to try and level up our careers in 2008 and small apartment life was a challenge for studio space. My messy nature didn't help!

2011

It was always important to me for my rig to be very mobile for working anywhere. Also at this point I had upgraded to a Mac Mini as seen in the photo.

2012

In 2012 I was fortunate enough to get some more space with a bigger desk, more screens and speakers, but OOF! So messy still! This setup spawned the beginnings of what would become Stars of Astraea.

2016

New Macbook Pro, better screen setup, and ALMOST an organized desk! You can also see the first iteration of my desktop PC in a GIANT case on the floor. I had no idea what I was doing so I bought a huge case thinking it would be better for airflow, turned out to be totally unnecessary.

2017

Lots of upgrades here! Matching screens, proper speaker monitors, and nice new 9' desk. Clean AF!

2018

Still keeping it clean, now featuring amazing artwork & d√©cor from my lovely wife. Somehow the DIGI002 is still going strong here as well 12 years later.

2020

In 2020 I made some significant upgrades, including new 4K screens and finally retiring the DIGI002 in favor of the Presonus 18|24. After all the years of sending money to Avid for Pro Tools, I also started experimenting with other Digital Audio Workstations such as Logic and REAPER. I quickly found that I preferred REAPER, and also started working with it more frequently on the desktop PC as my MacBook Pro was not aging well.

2022

And finally, here we are in 2022.
